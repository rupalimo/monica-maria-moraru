Not An Hour

  • Part of Earthbonds II
  • Curated by J.L. Murtaugh
  • Rupert Residency Center
  • Vilnius, Lithuania
  • May 5-8, 2024

Architectural form itself becomes a sensory organism in ‘Not An Hour’, a sculptural installation that folds sonic resonation into material form. Resonant frequencies emit vibrations through the structure’s steel plates, transferring subtle movements to a fabric membrane. A structural imposition activated as a sensorial tool, ‘Not An Hour’ responds to the environment through cracks, hums, and sensorial vibration through both dissonance and harmony. The structure’s formal references to Sovietic brutalism nods to the political and architectural history of the Baltic states, as the shelled and hollowed plates offer a haunting evocation of the skeletal remains of past regimes.

Commissioned by Rupert Residency Centre, Not an Hour explores architectural form as sensory organism, in the specificity of the post-Soviet context. The structure was equipped with a vibrational resonator which made contact with the structure’s steel plates, subtly tuning the metal, in turn transferring subtle movements to the work’s fabric membrane. responding to the environment through cracks, hums, and sensorial vibrations.

I Am Also Part of the Three Turns

Si Eu Fac Parte din Cele Trei Întorsături

  • 15min, 2024

I Am Also Part of the Three Turns traces the effects of a destructive earthquake in Bucharest and a concurrent flood it caused in a small town in Buzau, during a period of aggressive nationalistic urbanization in Communist Romania. Weaving between the allegorical and the archival, the film sketches a causal series of personal and political congruences throughout a patchwork of intertwined landscapes. Relying on oral and fragmentary archival histories, ‘I Am Also Part of the Three Turns’ teases out a temporally irresolute past that defies the linear cause-and-effect of both environmental disaster and authoritarian political repression.

An Ant In The Mouth of a Furnace

  • Curated by Bunker 2, Toronto, CA
  • As part of CONTACT Photography Festival
  • May 5 - 30, 2022

Integrating cast-glass sculptures, architectural scaffolding and multi-channeled sound, An Ant In The Mouth of a Furnace culminates into an architectural and experiential meditation on the phenomenological concerns of image-making. The site-specific installation was accompanied by a 4-channel sound work, arranged and scored with a film composer, culminating in a sensorial expression of an intimate architecture, at once reverent as a cathedral and provisional as a film set.
